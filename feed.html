  • Linear Mixed Models: A Practical Guide Using Statistical Software Brady T. West · ★★★ Apr 26, 2026
    I'm of two minds on how to rate this. On the one hand the writing is very clear, the book is extensive in its examples and discussion of the differences in statistical software. And I might have liked the book more if I actually needed any of the software in this book other than R. My low rating is largely due to the lack of practice problems at all. I think it is strange to have a technical book without any problem set whatsoever. I do not expect the book to have problems for doing the math per se, that is not really what the book is about. But the book would benefit greatly from very short "Check your understanding problems" or some high quality true or false questions. The chapter summaries are really halfhearted and hardly touch on any of the important topics discussed in the book. The authors are very knowledgeable and I've learned a lot from reading this book. I just wish they had made it easier for me to do so!

  • The Elements (The Elements, #1-4) John Boyne · ★★★★★ Apr 6, 2026
    The Elements is one of the most beautiful books I've ever read. The prose is immaculate, the story flows smoothly, and with every twist and turn in the story it's as if John Boyne tells you that he is playing with your feelings but you relish it all the same. I feel now like I know many of the characters and that I want them to find peace in their own way. And even if a character's journey is not explicitly in the pages I'd like to imagine that they found their peace. The book poses a few simple questions, a few of which come to mind: How do we handle our trauma? Should we let it take over our lives, lash out, become self-destructive, recoil from the ones we love? Do we just do our best to soldier on? Do we let others help us heal? One question posed directly in the book has been nagging at me: Is it easy to live without a conscience? At this moment my answer would be that it's harder in the short run but easier in the long run. Surely building up others is better for our soul than tearing others down. Wow what a beautiful book. Highly highly recommend this masterpiece.
